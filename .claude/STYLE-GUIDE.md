# Club Caddy - Writing Style Guide for Claude

## Language & Spelling

### Use New Zealand English
- **Colour** not color
- **Favourite** not favorite
- **Organise** not organize
- **Centre** not center
- **Customise** not customize
- **Analyse** not analyze
- **Travelled** not traveled
- **Metre** not meter (for measurements)
- **Programme** not program (except in computing context)

### Common NZ Terms
- **Mum** not mom
- **Petrol** not gas/gasoline
- **Boot** (car) not trunk
- **Bonnet** (car) not hood
- **Flat** not apartment
- **Holiday** not vacation
- **Mobile** not cell phone
- **Torch** not flashlight

## Grammar Rules

### NO Oxford Commas
Use New Zealand style without serial commas.

**Correct**: "We offer standard, premium and ultimate packages"
**Incorrect**: "We offer standard, premium, and ultimate packages"

**Correct**: "Choose from red, blue or green"
**Incorrect**: "Choose from red, blue, or green"

**Correct**: "Available for golf courses, resorts and private homes"
**Incorrect**: "Available for golf courses, resorts, and private homes"

### Minimise Hyphens
Avoid unnecessary hyphens in compound words.

**Prefer**: email, ecommerce, onsite, online, setup, checkout
**Avoid**: e-mail, e-commerce, on-site, on-line, set-up, check-out

**Keep hyphens only when necessary**:
- "72-volt battery" (technical specification)
- "high-performance cart" (adjective before noun)
- "6-seater" (number-noun combination)
- "all-wheel drive"

**Don't use hyphens**:
- "real time updates" (not real-time)
- "long term rental" (not long-term)
- "high quality products" (not high-quality)

## Tone & Voice

### Professional but Friendly
- Use active voice
- Keep sentences clear and concise
- Avoid excessive formality
- Write for New Zealand audience

### Product Descriptions
- Focus on benefits not just features
- Use specific numbers (100km+ range, 50km/h top speed)
- Emphasise luxury and quality
- Maintain professional tone

## Formatting

### Numbers
- Use metric measurements (km, kg, metres)
- Currency: NZD or $ (e.g., "$9,200 NZD" or "$9,200")
- Phone: +64-021-560-307
- Dates: DD/MM/YYYY (New Zealand format)

### Headings
- Use sentence case for most headings
- Title case only for main titles
- Keep concise

### Lists
- Use bullet points for features
- No Oxford commas in lists
- Parallel structure

## Examples

### Good (NZ English, no Oxford comma, fewer hyphens)
```
Our premium golf carts feature lithium batteries, touchscreen displays and
reverse cameras. Choose from multiple colours including pearl white, ocean
blue and forest green. We customise every cart to suit your requirements with
options for seating, battery type and accessories.

Available for immediate purchase or hire across New Zealand with delivery to
Auckland, Wellington and Christchurch.
```

### Bad (US English, Oxford commas, excessive hyphens)
```
Our premium golf-carts feature lithium-batteries, touch-screen displays, and
reverse-cameras. Choose from multiple colors including pearl-white, ocean-blue,
and forest-green. We customize every cart to suit your requirements with options
for seating, battery-type, and accessories.

Available for immediate purchase or rental across New-Zealand with delivery to
Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch.
```

## Technical Writing

### Code Comments
Use NZ English in comments and documentation.

```typescript
// Customise the cart configuration
// Organise products by colour and type
// Calculate total price in NZD
```

### Git Commits
Use NZ English in commit messages.

```
feat: add colour selector for customisation
fix: organise product catalogue by tier
docs: update pricing in NZD
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Don't Use
- ❌ "customize" → Use "customise"
- ❌ "color" → Use "colour"
- ❌ "organize" → Use "organise"
- ❌ "A, B, and C" → Use "A, B and C"
- ❌ "e-commerce" → Use "ecommerce"
- ❌ "on-line" → Use "online"
- ❌ "check-out" → Use "checkout"

### Do Use
- ✅ "customise"
- ✅ "colour"
- ✅ "organise"
- ✅ "A, B and C"
- ✅ "ecommerce"
- ✅ "online"
- ✅ "checkout"
